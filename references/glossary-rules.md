# Glossary Rules

Use this file when converting research and extracted strings into stable Korean terminology.

## Canonicality

Default to `global canonical` for:
- UI labels
- system prompts
- item families
- core gameplay terms
- place names and institutions

Allow `context-sensitive` variation only when:
- dialogue tone changes the natural Korean phrasing
- a repeated English line has different speaker intent
- rigid global reuse would sound unnatural in narrative text

Do not vary a term just to avoid repetition.

## Proper nouns

- Keep official names stable once confirmed.
- Transliterate only when translation harms recognition.
- Translate descriptive names when the Korean result stays natural and identifiable.
- Separate `confirmed` names from speculative names in the glossary.

## Repeated gameplay terms

Stabilize:
- action verbs
- menu labels
- item suffixes and prefixes
- rarity, quality, and status labels
- crafting and farming terminology

When item names form a family, record the family rule, not only the individual item.

## Safety rules

- Preserve placeholders, tags, and escapes exactly.
- Do not let a benchmark translation override stronger lore evidence.
- Do not let machine translation choose glossary terms or speaker tone. Use it only as a suspect draft and confirm against source meaning, extracted lore, and repeated in-game usage.
- Do not keep English fragments inside Korean sentences unless they are true product names, technical codes, or official brand terms.
- Prefer Korean phrasing over pseudo-fantasy English when meaning is clear.

## Feedback loop

Tester findings can promote glossary changes only when:
- the issue recurs
- the updated term improves clarity or tone
- the change does not break established canon elsewhere

Record both the chosen term and the rejected alternatives when the choice is non-obvious.
